Spicy Stir-Fried Water Spinach

Spicy Stir-Fried Water Spinach, also known as Kangkung Belacan in Malaysia and Indonesia or Pad Pak Boong Fai Daeng in Thailand, is a popular Southeast Asian dish made with water spinach (Ipomoea aquatica), a leafy green vegetable commonly found in tropical regions.

Ingredients

2 bunches of water spinach
4 shallots
2 cloves of garlic
1 cm galangal
1 bay leaf
1 tomato
6 chilies (ground)
100 ml water
1 tsp broth powder
1/2 tsp salt

How to make

First you have to heat the oil in the pan, then add the sliced shallots, garlic, bay leaf, and galangal. Stir-fry until soft and fragrant.
Add the ground chilies and sliced ingredients, then pour in the water. Stir well.
Add the water spinach and cook for only 3 minutes. Cover the pan and let it steam.
Once the water spinach is cooked, the dish is ready to be served.
